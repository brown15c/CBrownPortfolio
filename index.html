About

General Overview of Past Experiences

  • 2013-2015

    North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics

    At this prestigious high school, I was allowed to explore many medias of art and design from fashion design to cinematography and develop my initial interest in architecture.

  • June-July, 2014

    Diving into Architecture

    Attending Summer Pre-College at Cornell University was an eye-opening experience as I gained condensed exposure to studio culture, college level architecture and design processes, and allowed me to confirm that I truly wanted study this major.

  • 2014-2015

    Research of What I Love

    After Cornell, I was determined to go back to high school and continue architecture. I began research in Architecture at my school with Duda Paine Architects and investigated methods of improving the school campus to be more sustainable.

  • October, 2015- Currently

    Carnegie Mellon University

    Now I currently study at the School of Architecture at Carnegie Mellon University and am constantly learning more about architecture and the world around us.

Rewind

Sustainable Fashion Design that was showcased in the NCSSM Multicultural Fashion Show in 2014.

This outfit is created out of trashed film strips and traditional photographs from the art studio on campus, hot glue, and recycled fabric. The film strips were hot pressed flat to change them from their original curled state. The outfit was showcased under the sustainability theme for the Multicultural Fashion Show.

  • Model: Christina Brown
  • Date: March, 2014
  • Category: Fashion Design

Over My Head

Digital Media Work in 2014

This artwork is created digitally inspired by the phrase "I'm in over my head" taken in a more literal sense. I find the exploration of taking phrases in a more literal sense to be quite interesting since it demonstrates the process of how such language is originally created.

Date: January, 2014

Tool Extrapolation Project

Tool investigation and extrapolation project at Cornell Summer College, summer of 2014.

After the exploration and investigation of a portable hand saw, I created a tool relief model which was then extrapolated and translated into a space of contemplation on the side of a gorge. Models are made of chipboard.

Date:June-July, 2015

Pride

Makeup design inspired by the legalization of Gay Marriage within the United States in 2015.

This makeup design was inspired and in celebration of the legalization of gay marriage in the United States. The rainbow strips was painted with normal washable paint and eye shadow. Photography was done with no special lighting.

Model: Jessi Murray

Date: July, 2015

Left||Right

Exploration of left brain versus right brain concept

I wanted to explore the common psychology concept of right brain versus left brain where one side is more artistically inclined and the other is more mathematically inclined. This drawing explores that idea to the hyper extent through ditial media.

Date: September, 2015

Presidence House Exploration of the Miller House

Exploration of the Miller House by Jose Oubrerie.

This project is the first major project at Carnegie Mellon University where we investigated the Miller House and recreated the house model in basswood.

Date: October, 2015
